Product Overview

Plinest® PN HPT® is part of a wider family of Polynucleotide High Purification Technology (PN HPT®) products developed by Mastelli, an Italian pharmaceutical and aesthetic medicine company founded in Sanremo in 1952. Plinest® was the first PN HPT®-based medical device registered for intradermal injection, originally introduced in 2005, and has since been supported by more than 100 published scientific studies.

PN HPT® is derived from highly purified deoxyribonucleotide polymers (DNA fractions) sourced from salmon trout bred in controlled European fish farms. Through Mastelli's in-house high-purification process, these polynucleotides are refined into a sterile, non-pyrogenic, viscoelastic gel intended for intradermal administration. Plinest® is positioned as a bio-regenerative treatment rather than a structural dermal filler — its intended role is to support the skin's own regenerative processes, including fibroblast activity associated with collagen production, rather than to add volume.

Plinest® is classified by the manufacturer as a medical device for professional use. It must be administered only by appropriately qualified and trained healthcare professionals, in line with the manufacturer's current instructions for use and applicable local regulations.

Technology

How PN HPT® Polynucleotide Technology Works

Plinest® is built on Polynucleotide High Purification Technology (PN HPT®) — Mastelli's proprietary in-house process for extracting and purifying deoxyribonucleotide polymers from salmon trout sourced through controlled European fish farms.

Skin Quality Support

PN HPT® is intended to support overall skin quality, including the appearance of radiance and freshness, rather than to add structural volume.

Bio-Regeneration

The formulation is designed to promote bio-regenerative processes in the skin, supporting fibroblast activity associated with collagen production.

Skin Priming

Mastelli describes a "priming" concept in which PN HPT® may prepare the skin to be more receptive to other medical-aesthetic procedures, including laser, radiofrequency, dermal fillers, peeling, needling and surgery.

Fibrous Tissue Remodelling

Plinest® may help to remodel areas with a higher fibrous tissue content, such as selected acne scars, subject to professional assessment.

PN HPT® treatments are administered intradermally, generally using a micro-droplet or linear retrograde injection technique, depending on the specific product and treatment area. Areas of treatment vary by formulation and may include the face, neck and décolleté (Plinest® and Newest®) or the eye contour (Plinest® Eye). Treatment protocols, session intervals and maintenance schedules should always follow the manufacturer's current instructions for use and the treating clinician's professional judgement.

Clinical Applications

Where Plinest® May Be Considered in Practice

Plinest® PN HPT® treatments are intended to support a range of skin-quality concerns commonly seen in aesthetic medicine practice. Suitability for any individual patient depends on professional clinical assessment.

01

Prevention & Maintenance of Skin Quality

Suggested for patients seeking to support younger-looking skin quality and to help maintain the skin's appearance over time, as part of a planned treatment course with maintenance sessions.

02

Recovery Treatment for Ageing Skin

A recovery-focused protocol intended for ageing skin, supporting the appearance of skin texture, hydration and elasticity as part of a structured infiltration and maintenance schedule.

03

Selected Acne Scar Concerns

May be considered by the treating professional for fibrous or atrophic acne-scar areas, where remodelling of higher fibrous-content tissue is a treatment objective.

04

Periocular Skin Quality (Plinest® Eye)

Plinest® Eye is intended specifically for the periocular region, supporting skin texture, firmness and elasticity in this delicate area.

05

Mature or Dehydrated Skin (Newest®)

Newest® combines PN HPT® with hyaluronic acid, suggested for mature or dehydrated skin across extended areas including the face, neck and décolleté.

06

Skin Priming Ahead of Other Procedures

PN HPT® priming may be used by the treating professional ahead of other aesthetic procedures such as laser, radiofrequency, filler treatment, peeling or needling, as part of an integrated treatment plan.
